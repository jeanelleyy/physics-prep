Speed Vs. Velocity

Speed is a scalar quantity, meaning it has no direction. Velocity, on the other hand, is a vector quantity and has direction. The difference between their formulas lie in the distance/displacement variable. If the variable being divided by time is distance, then that is the formula for speed. Dividing displacement with time will give you velocity.
